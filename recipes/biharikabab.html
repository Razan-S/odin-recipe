Bihari Kabab

Description

These uniquely tender, aromatic, and smoky kababs remind me of my childhood in Pakistan.

The Ingredients

  • Kabab
    • Beef tenderloin
    • Avocado oil
    • Onion
    • Kiwi
    • Flour
    • Poppy seeds
    • Garlic paste
    • Ginger paste
    • Chile pepper
    • Mustard oil
    • White vinegar
    • Yogurt
  • Spices
    • Whole nutmeg seed
    • Whole mace
    • Anise pods
    • Whole dried red Kashmiri chilies
    • Chili powder
    • Ground cumin
    • Garam masala
    • Freshly ground black pepper
    • Salt

The way to make

  1. Pound tenderloin strips with a meat mallet until they are very thin; set aside.
  2. Fry onion in the hot avocado oil until it is a deep brown
  3. Peel kiwi, cut into quarters, and set aside in a small bowl for later.
  4. Toast gram flour until lightly browned and nutty, transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  5. Dry-roast poppy seeds in the same skillet until lightly brown and aromatic, 2 to 4 minutes. Remove to a small bowl; set aside.
  6. In the same skillet, dry-roast nutmeg, mace, star anise, and Kashmiri chilies together until fragrant
  7. Transfer the whole spices and chilies to a spice grinder, and grind them to a coarse powder.
  8. Add browned onions, kiwi, ginger paste, garlic paste, and serrano chile to the bowl of a food processor, and grind to a paste
  9. Pour in mustard oil, white vinegar, and yogurt and pulse the food processor until everything is well blended
  10. Add roasted poppy seeds, roasted gram flour, roasted ground whole spices, chili powder, cumin, garam masala, black pepper, and salt to the food processor
  11. Spoon marinade into a resealable plastic bag and add tenderized beef strips.
  12. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours
  13. Remove marinated beef from the refrigerator, thread beef strips on sturdy metal skewers, packing them in tightly.
  14. Grill kababs on the preheated grill
  15. Turn kebabs, brush with marinade, and continue cooking until the internal temperature of the meat reaches 170 degrees F
  16. Remove skewers to a serving dish. Discard any uncooked marinade. Enjoy the kababs with naan, sliced red onions, tamarind chutney, and green chutney.
